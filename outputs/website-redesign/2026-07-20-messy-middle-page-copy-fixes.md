## 3. "Rolling basis" — RESOLVED: keep it, and add the close date

Jackie confirmed 2026-07-20 that **rolling review is deliberate** — reviewing applications as they arrive is easier for her than batching them. Keep the page line exactly as it is:

> Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis. You'll hear from me within 2 business days.

The earlier concern was overstated. A close date and rolling review answer two different questions: *"by when must I apply?"* and *"how fast will I hear back?"* Both can be true at once, and together they're stronger than either alone — apply by the 25th, hear back within two days.

**The one thing to add**, near the apply button:

> Applications for the October cohort close Friday, September 25. The cohort begins Friday, October 9.

Without a date somewhere on the page, "rolling basis" reads as *there's no rush* and quietly undoes the urgency the email is built on.

**A real benefit worth noting:** rolling review spreads intake across the entire application window instead of stacking it into the two weeks before kickoff. That materially reduces the mid-term crunch that drove pulling the deadline back to Sept 25 — the deadline is still the right call, but the load is now flatter than feared.
