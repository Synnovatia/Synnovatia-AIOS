# LinkedIn Marketing

> Built 2026-07-12. Governs content, outreach, and engagement on LinkedIn. Voice follows `context/brand-voice.md` throughout — peer not guru, precise not generic, warm not soft, confident not boastful, equip not help.

---

## Goals

All of the following, in priority order matching `context/strategy.md`:
1. Attract new 1:1/retainer clients (target: 5 new monthly clients in 3 months)
2. Fill Seven Figure Forum (target: 6 members by Jan 2027) and Mastermind for the Messy Middle (target: 8-10 by Oct 2026)
3. General visibility for the "Different Is Better Than Better" rebrand

## Ideal Customer Profile (for outreach/prospecting)

- **Role:** Founder, CEO, Owner, President — the actual decision-maker, not an employee
- **Business:** B2B service business
- **Revenue:** $250K–$4M (Stage II / "Messy Middle" per brand positioning) — within that, $250K-$500K skews toward Messy Middle mastermind fit, $1M+ skews toward Seven Figure Forum fit
- **Team size:** Typically 5-10 people (mix of employees/subcontractors)
- **Funding:** Bootstrapped — no VC/PE/angel capital (this is a core brand-voice distinction, not incidental)
- **Signal to look for:** LinkedIn posts/bio language about feeling stuck, stalled growth, "wearing too many hats," wanting outside perspective — this is "Messy Middle" language, the audience's actual felt experience

## Content Pillars & Cadence

**Posts go live 3x/week — Monday, Wednesday, Friday, 7:30am Pacific**, via LinkedIn's own "schedule post" feature (Jackie queues them herself — Claude never posts or schedules anything directly).

**Drafting happens in one batch on Fridays, 9am Pacific** — Claude drafts all three upcoming posts at once, Jackie reviews and schedules them via LinkedIn.

| Day | Pillar | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Thought leadership | A pattern/insight from 27 years of cross-industry consulting. Precise, not generic — name the revenue range/stage per voice rules. |
| Wednesday | Story | Personal or client story that humanizes the "peer, not guru" positioning. Warmth through specificity, not platitudes. |
| Friday | Client win | A real result or transformation (anonymized/generalized unless a client has explicitly agreed to be named — check before naming anyone). Confident, not boastful — let the result speak. |

## Outreach / Connection Request Templates

**Template 1 — Cold connection, ICP match found via search/content:**
> Hi [Name] — saw your [post/profile] about [specific detail]. I work with B2B service business owners navigating exactly that stretch — stalled growth, too many hats, ready for a real outside perspective. Would love to connect.

**Template 2 — Warmer, engaged with content first:**
> Hi [Name] — really resonated with what you said about [specific point from their post]. That's the exact "Messy Middle" moment I spend my days helping owners navigate. Would love to connect and follow your journey.

**Template 3 — Mastermind-specific (for Seven Figure Forum / Messy Middle prospects):**
> Hi [Name] — I run two small mastermind groups for B2B service owners around your revenue range ([$1M+ / $250K-$500K]) navigating growth and scaling. No pitch — just wanted to connect given what you're building at [Company].

**Follow-up (after connection accepted, no reply needed to first message):**
> Thanks for connecting, [Name]! Curious — what's the biggest thing on your plate right now as you grow [Company]? Always love hearing how other owners are thinking through the messy middle stretch.

## Commenting/Engagement Guidance

Claude doesn't have live visibility into Jackie's LinkedIn feed. When Jackie shares what she's seeing (a post, a thread), Claude can draft comment angles — but this stays reactive, initiated by Jackie.

**What makes a good comment (per brand voice):**
- Add a specific insight or ask a real question — not "Great post!" generic engagement
- Reference the actual revenue stage/scenario when relevant (precise, not generic)
- Sound like a peer who's been there, not a consultant pitching services

## Hard Boundaries

- **No browser automation on LinkedIn.** Posting, commenting, and connecting are all manual — LinkedIn detects and restricts automated account behavior. This protects Jackie's actual account standing.
- **No auto-posting.** Every draft is reviewed by Jackie before it goes anywhere.
- **Client wins:** never name a client without confirming they're comfortable being named — default to anonymized/generalized.
