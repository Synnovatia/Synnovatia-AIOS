## Positioning Thesis

In a market where competitors race to be *better* along the same dimensions, Synnovatia wins by being *different* — a competitive philosophy, not a tagline (per Craig Ullom, external marketing observer, validating the thesis from outside the client base).

The differentiation lives at the intersection of:
- **Fortune 500-grade strategic expertise**, and
- **Genuine peer-level relational depth**

Neither element alone is the differentiator — it's the combination clients can't find anywhere else.

## Audience

Stage II entrepreneurs · **$250K–$4M revenue** · navigating the **"Messy Middle"** — stalled growth, resource constraints, strategic complexity. *(Corrected 2026-07-13 from $350K–$4M per the official 2026 Style Guide — the $250K floor matches the actual Messy Middle mastermind band.)*

**Messaging architecture rule (Craig Ullom):** Lead with the customer's problem, not the solution. Buyers don't start out looking for a coach — they start out feeling stuck. Meet them there first.

## Voice Rules

| Dimension | Do This | Not This |
|---|---|---|
| Peer, not guru | Sound like a fellow business owner who has been there | Sound like a platform or methodology vendor |
| Precise, not generic | Name the revenue range ($250K–$4M) and the stage (Messy Middle) | Use broad language like "small business owners" or "entrepreneurs" |
| Warm, not soft | Let warmth come through specificity and client language | Use motivational-speaker tropes or vague encouragement |
| Confident, not boastful | Let clients make the strong claims; Synnovatia frames the context | Lead with credentials or methodology before the client's problem |
| Equip, not help | Synnovatia *equips* Stage II owners to navigate the Messy Middle | Use the word "help" — it undersells the strategic depth of the work |

## Additional Messaging Principles (Craig Ullom, external observer)

- **Video as a trust accelerator** — a prospect who's watched Jackie on video (genuine, not produced) arrives to a discovery call already partially sold. The authenticity gap between polished copy and a real person on camera is where trust gets built.
- **Buyer decision-style targeting** — some buyers move on logic/ROI, others on story/identity, others need social proof. Don't assume one message resonates universally.
- **The discovery call is the conversion moment** — per Mark Chapman's interview, trust was earned in the first meeting, not through marketing copy. Design early client touchpoints (discovery calls, first sessions) as deliberate proof-of-concept experiences, not just intake.

## Locked CTA Copy

- **Nav:** "Let's Have a Conversation"
- **Body/Closing:** "Start the Conversation"
- **Mastermind:** "Apply for Consideration"
- **Booking:** "Find a Time That Works"

## Avoid (AI-Tell Policy)

- Arrow glyphs on CTAs, "Let's [verb] →" microcopy
- Boxed trait pills
- 3-column testimonial grids
- Per-section eyebrows (max two per page)
- Anaphoric/negation-heavy cadence
