## Positioning Thesis

In a market where competitors race to be *better* along the same dimensions, Synnovatia wins by being *different* — a competitive philosophy, not a tagline (per Craig Ullom, external marketing observer).

The differentiation lives at the intersection of:
- **Fortune 500-grade strategic expertise**, and
- **Genuine peer-level relational depth**

Neither element alone is the differentiator — it's the combination clients can't find anywhere else.

## Audience

Stage II entrepreneurs · **$350K–$4M revenue** · navigating the **"Messy Middle"** — stalled growth, resource constraints, strategic complexity.

**Messaging architecture rule:** Lead with the customer's problem, not the solution. Buyers start out stuck — not searching for a coach. Meet them there first.

## Voice Rules

| Dimension | Do This | Not This |
|---|---|---|
| Peer, not guru | Sound like a fellow business owner who has been there | Sound like a platform or methodology vendor |
| Precise, not generic | Name the revenue range ($350K–$4M) and the stage (Messy Middle) | Use broad language like "small business owners" or "entrepreneurs" |
| Warm, not soft | Let warmth come through specificity and client language | Use motivational-speaker tropes or vague encouragement |
| Confident, not boastful | Let clients make the strong claims; Synnovatia frames the context | Lead with credentials or methodology before the client's problem |
| Equip, not help | Synnovatia *equips* Stage II owners to navigate the Messy Middle | Use the word "help" — it undersells the strategic depth of the work |

## Messaging Pillars — Strongest Proof Points

- **Outside Objectivity** — "I was too close to see my own situation objectively." — Mark Chapman
- **30,000-Ft Strategic View** — "Jackie helps me see the 30,000-ft view." — Raffi Saroyan
- **Warmth as Strategic Asset** — Not incidental — load-bearing. Warmth most competitors can't manufacture. — Laura Labovich
- **Practical Collaboration, Not Cheerleading** — Rolls up her sleeves; delivers something practical, not just encouragement. — Naya Troup
- **Emotional Intelligence** — Holds space for the ache and frustration, and knows when it's time to move on. — Christina Carlson
- **Solopreneur Advisory Scarcity** — "There's no one else doing what Jackie does, at this level, for solopreneurs." — Raffi Saroyan

## Locked CTA Copy

- **Nav:** "Let's Have a Conversation"
- **Body/Closing:** "Start the Conversation"
- **Mastermind:** "Apply for Consideration"
- **Booking:** "Find a Time That Works"

## Avoid (AI-Tell Policy)

- Arrow glyphs on CTAs, "Let's [verb] →" microcopy
- Boxed trait pills
- 3-column testimonial grids
- Per-section eyebrows (max two per page)
- Anaphoric/negation-heavy cadence
